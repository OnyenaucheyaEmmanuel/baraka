Statement of Philosophy

As an advocate for early childhood education, I believe in fostering a nurturing and inclusive environment where every child is valued, respected, and empowered to reach their full potential. My core beliefs center around the importance of child-centered learning, holistic development, and collaborative partnerships.I believe in honoring each child's unique interests, strengths, and learning styles, and fostering a curriculum that is responsive and relevant to their individual needs. I am committed to supporting children's cognitive, social-emotional, physical, and creative growth, recognizing the interconnectedness of these domains in shaping their overall development.

Approaches

I embrace play-based learning as a foundation for children's exploration, discovery, and self-expression, providing opportunities for hands-on, experiential learning that sparks curiosity and fosters a love for learning. I believe in an emergent curriculum approach, where learning emerges from children's interests, questions, and experiences, allowing for flexibility and responsiveness to children's evolving needs and interests. I strive to be a responsive and reflective educator, adapting my teaching practices to meet the diverse needs of each child, providing scaffolding and support as they navigate their learning journey.

Exploring Patterns in Nature

Grade Level: Preschoolers
Integrated Learning: Arts, Science, Language Arts, Mathematics
Duration: 45 Minutes

Learning Objectives:

Identify and describe natural materials.
Build Vocabulary related to natural materials.
Engage in counting and sorting activities.
Practice cooperative play amd collaboration.

Materials Needed:

Various natural materials such as leaves, flowers, sticks, rocks and feathers.
Large sheets of paper or cardboard.
Markers or Crayon.
Glue.

Procedure

Introduction:

Start by taking the children on a short nature walk, within the school environment. Encourage them to collect intersting natural materials they find along the way.

Main Activity 1: Creative Art

Once back indoors, I provided each pupil with a large sheet of paper or cardboard as their canvas.
Showed them how to arrange and glue the collected materials onto the paper to create a collage. Encouraged them to use their imagination and creativity to design patterns, shapes or even scenes.

Main Activity 2: Collaborative Work

I let the children experiment freely with the materials, offering guidance and support as needed.
After the collage were completed, I encouraged the children to share their creations with each other, explaining the choices they made and the stories behind their artwork.

Main Activity 3: Counting and Building

Ask them to count the numer of natural materialsthey gathered along the way.
This Integrates maths concept while reinforcing the idea of natural art.

Closure and Reflection

The Children use markers or crayons to add extra details or designs to their collage.
This activity encourages the children to explore nature,use their imagination and develop fine motor skills through arranging and gluing materials. It also provided an opportunity for self-expression and creativity as they design their own unique collages.
